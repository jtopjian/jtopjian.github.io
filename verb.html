Verb

Verb is an AUv3 audio effect for iOS with three driven algorithmic reverb engines for techno and industrial production.

Quick start

  1. Open your Audio Unit host.
  2. Add Verb as an AUv3 audio effect to your track or bus.
  3. Choose an engine: CONCRETE, GATE PIT, or BLOOM RUST.
  4. Set ROOM and DECAY, then blend with MIX and OUTPUT.
Note: Verb processes audio. Insert it directly on an audio or instrument track.

Knob guide

ENGINE
Concrete = tight/dark, Gate Pit = gated punch, Bloom Rust = reverse swells
ROOM
Morphs from smaller rooms to wider spaces inside the selected engine
DECAY
Controls reverb tail length
TONE
Dark (low) to bright (high) wet-path tone shaping
DRIVE / GRIT
Saturation and lo-fi degradation on the reverb character
CLAMP / STAGGER
Tempo-aware tail gating and rhythmic re-clamp pulses
MIX / OUTPUT
Wet/dry blend and final output trim

Common questions

  • Too muddy: Lower ROOM, cut PRE LOW, and trim POST LOW.
  • Too harsh: Lower TONE and POST HIGH, then reduce DRIVE if needed.
  • Clamp feels inactive: CLAMP follows host tempo when available; increase CLAMP and STAGGER for stronger rhythmic action.
  • No audible effect: Raise MIX above dry, confirm DECAY is not minimal, and check OUTPUT.
  • High CPU usage: Verb is moderate CPU; reduce stacked effects in the signal chain if needed.
